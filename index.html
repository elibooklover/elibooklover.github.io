Hoyeol Kim

CV


I am a Digital Humanist, Data Scientist, and Victorianist. I am currently a PhD candidate in English at Texas A&M University. I received a MA in English Literary Studies from the University of Exeter and a BA in English Literature (minor in Linguistics) from the Hankuk University of Foreign Studies.

I first learned how to code when I was 8 years old, and practiced coding by making computer games similar to Galaga. I passed my first computer certification exam when I was 9 years old. I began to learn English and Japanese when I was 12 years old, and I passed the N3 JLPT(Japanese-Language Proficiency Test) when I was 16 years old. I was into computer software and hardware when I was young, and I started to be immersed in literature when I was in high school.

Although I was disciplined in the humanities at universities, I was also trained at IT companies by co-workers who worked for Samsung, LG, or HP. I deal with computer programming/markup languages and frameworks such as R, Swift, Python, SAS, JSON, HTML, CSS, TEI/XML, Jekyll, WordPress, Django, PyTorch, Keras, and TensorFlow. I am interested in working in interdisciplinary studies by combining literature and computer/data science.

I presented my work on sentiment analysis packages at ACH 2019 in Pittsburgh during July, 2019. I presented my paper, “Digital Analysis of Dickensian Characters and the Language of Emotion,” at the 2018 Dickens Society Symposium in Tübingen, Germany, during the summer of 2018. I also attended the Digital Humanities Summer Institute at the University of Victoria in the summer of 2018 with funding from both CoDHR and the Department of English at Texas A&M University. In the Spring of 2021, I am teaching ENGL 304: Topics in Digital Research at Texas A&M University.

I am currently working on my PhD dissertation “Computational Approaches in the Humanities from Sentiment Analysis to Machine Learning Colorization.” I am the creator of the Victorian400 dataset for colorizing black-and-white nineteenth-century illustrations using deep learning and the VictorianLit dataset for machine learning-based sentiment analysis of Victorian literary texts.

I also have some publications, including an academic article, and have participated in digital projects for three years, during which I helped to publish the history of British Kings and Queens and an online literary encyclopedia: Queen Victoria in NAVER History Encyclopedia (NAVER is Korea’s most popular search engine, which also developed the LINE app). I have also worked on commercial mobile apps. Namely, K-Town Rewards in DC and Vinny’s 17 on the App Store and Google Play, and Tulango on Google Play.

If you would like to chat, please feel free to contact me via email or Twitter.
